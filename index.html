The Views Golf Club — Google Ads Analysis

Oro Valley, AZ  ·  11-month campaign

This tool was built to answer a simple question: if The Views Golf Club ran a Google Search Ads campaign, could it profitably generate new golfers? The starting hypothesis was that acquiring 4 new golfers per day over an 11-month season would represent meaningful, measurable growth. Working backward from Google Ads benchmarks for golf search campaigns in southern Arizona — typical cost-per-click and conversion rates — a monthly budget of $1,000 emerged as the baseline that makes that target achievable at roughly $8.33 per acquired golfer. With a $70 round price, that is an 8.4× return on ad spend before a single golfer comes back for a second round. The sliders below let you stress-test that baseline against different budgets and round prices.

Is this economical?

At $8.33 cost per golfer vs $70 revenue, the baseline ROAS is 8.4×. Every dollar above $1,000/month delivers more golfers at the same unit cost — the economics hold at any budget level.

Budget scales golfers linearly. $2,000/month → 8 golfers/day. $1,500/month → 6/day. Cost per golfer stays constant at $8.33 as you scale.

Key risk: The $8.33 cost-per-golfer assumes consistent CPC and conversion rate. Set up conversion tracking (GolfNow, TeeSnap, or direct booking) before launch so you can validate against real data.
