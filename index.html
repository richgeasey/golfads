The Views Golf Club — Google Ads Analysis

Oro Valley, AZ  ·  11-month campaign

This tool was built to answer a simple question: if The Views Golf Club ran a Google Search Ads campaign, could it profitably generate new golfers? The starting hypothesis was that acquiring 4 new golfers per day over an 11-month season would represent meaningful, measurable growth. Working backward from Google Ads benchmarks for golf search campaigns in southern Arizona — typical cost-per-click and conversion rates — a monthly budget of $1,000 emerged as the baseline that makes that target achievable at roughly $8.33 per acquired golfer. With a $70 round price, that is an 8.4× return on ad spend before a single golfer comes back for a second round. Is this economical?

At $8.33 cost per golfer vs $70 revenue, the baseline ROAS is 8.4×. Every dollar above $1,000/month delivers more golfers at the same unit cost — the economics hold at any budget level.

Budget scales golfers linearly. $2,000/month → 8 golfers/day. $1,500/month → 6/day. Cost per golfer stays constant at $8.33 as you scale.

Key risk: The $8.33 cost-per-golfer assumes consistent CPC and conversion rate. Set up conversion tracking (GolfNow, TeeSnap, or direct booking) before launch so you can validate against real data.

Beyond paid ads — organic golfer acquisition

The Google Ads campaign establishes a reliable, measurable baseline — $1,000/month delivers 4 new golfers per day at a proven cost of $8.33 per acquisition. But paid search is only one channel. The Views Golf Club website and Google Business Profile represent two additional acquisition levers that cost nothing per click and compound over time. Here is an honest assessment of where each stands today and what incremental golfers per day a focused improvement effort could realistically add.

Website & landing page

The current home page at theviewsgolfclub.com has a genuine visual asset — the Catalina Mountain setting and course photography — but it underperforms as a conversion tool for new golfers arriving from search. The primary CTA ("Book a Tee Time") exists but competes with membership-oriented navigation, and the headline "See the Views, Play the Views" is evocative without being search-relevant. For public golfers searching "tee times Oro Valley" or "golf course Tucson," the page does not immediately confirm it is the right place.

No geo-modified keywords in the H1 or opening paragraph. "Oro Valley" appears deep in the body copy — Google and arriving visitors can't immediately confirm this is the course they searched for.

No visible pricing or rates on the home page. First-time visitors deciding whether to book have to click away to find rates — a proven friction point that increases bounce rate.

The tee time booking link routes to a subdomain with no visual continuity. The abrupt change in site design and URL at viewsgolfaz.cps.golf adds drop-off risk at the most critical moment in the conversion funnel.

No reviews, star ratings, or social proof above the fold. Trust signals are absent exactly where they matter most — at the moment a new visitor is deciding whether to stay or leave.

The phone number is clickable but not obviously so. Nothing about its appearance signals that to a mobile visitor — it looks like static text. A styled button with a phone icon and a label like "Call to Book — Available 6 AM to 5 PM" makes the action obvious and sets expectations on when someone will answer, reducing the chance a golfer hits voicemail and abandons. Surfacing the hours makes the option feel reliable rather than ambiguous.

No explicit booking channel hierarchy. The page should present two clear paths: book online instantly 24/7, or call during business hours. Golfers arriving after 5 PM should see the online booking option front and center rather than a phone number they can't act on until morning.

Estimated impact: +0.5 to +1.0 golfers/day. A focused landing page built for search traffic — geo-relevant headline, current rates, one prominent online booking CTA, a clearly labeled tap-to-call button with hours, and 3–5 customer reviews — could improve conversion rate by 1.5–2 percentage points without increasing ad spend.

Local SEO

The Views has meaningful local equity — a distinctive name, active social profiles, and footer NAP. However, several high-impact local ranking factors are missing or weak, suppressing organic visibility for the search terms that matter most.

Title tag reads "Home." No course name, no city, no keyword. This is the single highest-leverage SEO fix on the entire site and takes under five minutes to change.

No LocalBusiness schema markup. Google cannot reliably extract structured address, hours, or service type from the page, which limits eligibility for rich results and local pack placement.

No Google Map embed, no explicit service area. "Oro Valley," "northwest Tucson," and "Marana" are natural geo-modifier targets that don't appear in the page copy in a structured way.

Estimated impact: +0.5 to +1.5 golfers/day. Local SEO compounds slowly — expect 3–6 months — but the gains are durable and reduce long-term dependence on paid spend. Fixing the title tag and adding schema are quick wins with outsized payoff.

Google Business Profile

The GBP listing appears active and has reviews, which is a genuine competitive advantage. However, there are likely profile gaps that suppress local map pack visibility and reduce click-through from golfers who find the listing.

Tee time booking not connected via Reserve with Google. Golfers who find the GBP listing have to navigate away to book — connecting the booking link directly in the profile removes that friction entirely.

Q&A section likely unanswered or unpopulated. Pre-populating Q&A with common questions (rates, hours, dress code, cart fees) is a direct conversion and ranking signal that most courses ignore.

Service attributes and amenities may be incomplete. Driving range, pro shop, lessons, and pull cart availability all affect how the profile appears in filtered local searches.

Estimated impact: +0.3 to +0.8 golfers/day. GBP gains are the fastest of the three organic channels — a fully optimized profile with active photos, answered Q&A, and consistent review responses can show measurable improvement in 30–60 days.
